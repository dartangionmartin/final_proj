Legendary Players

Jackie Robinson

Debuted in 1947 and broke MLB's color barrier. Robinson combined elite talent with tremendous courage and became a symbol for civil rights and progress.

Sandy Koufax

Koufax dominated the 1960s with multiple Cy Young Awards and a historic 1965 World Series performance, cementing his place as one of the greatest pitchers.

Fernando Valenzuela

Valenzuela sparked 'Fernandomania' in the early 1980s, inspiring fans in Los Angeles and around the world with his charisma and pitching.

Clayton Kershaw

Kershaw has been a modern ace for the Dodgers, earning Cy Young Awards, MVP honors, and anchoring the team’s pitching for more than a decade.
